Safety Measures

For protecting yourself

Thoroughly clean your hands. Use alcohol-based hand rub or soap. This kills viruses that may be on your hands.

Maintain at least 6 feet between yourself and others. When someone coughs, sneezes, or speaks they spread small infectious droplets. If you are too close, you may breathe in these droplets and become infected with COVID-19 if the person is a carrier.

Avoid crowded places. Harder to maintain distance of 6 feet, more likely to have close contact with someone that has COVID-19.

Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth with your hand. Viruses enter your body through these places if you touch them with contaminated hands.

To Protect the People around you

When you cough or sneeze, use your bent elbow or a tissue to cover your mouth and nose. Afterwards, immediately and wash your hands. If you are infected, droplets spread virus.

Self-isolate if you have minor symptoms (cough, headache, mild fever). Have someone deliver you supplies. If you must leave your house, be sure to wear a mask. Avoid contact with others to protect them from possible COVID-19.

If you have a fever, cough and difficulty breathing, seek medical attention by calling in advance. Calling in advance allows the health care workers to direct you to the right health facility. This not only protects you but also helps prevent the spread of the virus.

Get the latest information from trusted sources. Some examples include WHO or your local and national health authorities.

Source: World Health Organization
